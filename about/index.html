I think open source is not the future, but present. Lack of open source options in building industry makes harder to small companies to overcome the difficulties of modern world. That is why I have developed a series of civil engineering oriented open source tools among which I like to highlight 2. The first is a full geometry properties calculator (as inertia moment, areas, centroids, etc.) available here. It works in any browser and android devices. The second one is an interactive interaction diagram app for reinforced concrete columns. The interface allows a non-coding user to create a full 3D surface of interaction for any column with any rebar distribution. This tool is available here

Since 2018 to 2020 I worked as a geomatics academic tutor under the leadership of Ricardo Camacho and Orlando Clavijo. In three periods I developed advanced GIS abilities, mastering the ArcGIS suite for civil engineer related applications.
In these semesters I learned from one of the best in his area, Mr. Camilo Lizarazo, who guides me through the beautiful world of topography.


2 semesters ago I started working for Fernando Ramírez as an numerical analysis tutor. My work in NA is to assist students and the development of MANLets.


MANLets is an educational-focused programming project. The objective is to design, develop, and evaluate interactive numerical methods applets for students.
These tools will make the learning process easier, allowing students to interact and play with numerical methods from a non-coding perspective. MANLets include the implementation of several numerical methods and the integration of frameworks (as MathQuill, Plotly, Math.js, etc) in a complete web-based platform.
